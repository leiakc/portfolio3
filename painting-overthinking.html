Acrylic on Canvas • 30" x 30" • 2017

We all overthink. Simple questions like “what should I have for lunch?” to life-changing questions such as “should I change my career?” can swarm in our minds, making our lives chaotic. The chaos of overthinking is conveyed through expressive brushwork, symbolism, and composition. The artwork was inspired by Neo-Expressionism with its “spur of the moment” creation process and expressive brush strokes.

Without much planning of the artwork beforehand, I was in new territory. There is no space to overthink in Neo Expressionism: you jump right in with your idea and painting on the canvas - if you don’t like what you’ve done, you change it. The process evolves as you go along. At first, the process was nerve-wracking, but later I found it liberating. This artwork opens a space for people to discuss their overthinking; we can vent, listen to others, share solutions, and receive advice.

The creation of this artwork supports the idea of going with the flow and feeling out situations instead of getting stuck in overthinking, which we do as a means for control and security. After all, if we planned everything we did, where’s the space to live in the moment and experience the wonder of the unexpected?
